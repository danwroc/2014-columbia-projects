Top Architectural Sights to See in 2014

Old and new, short and tall, and spanning many styles, the architectural wonders of the world are stunning, but also sometimes forgotten travel destinations. Scroll down our list of the top 10 architectural wonders to see the beautiful structures that you won't want to miss this year.


10. Chichén Itzá, México

Built by the Mayans around 900 AD, the city displays many prominent elements of the architectural style of the times, incorporating both the Puuc and Chenes traditions. Visitors are no longer allowed to climb the stairs of the pyramids or go inside the ruins, but can still appreciate both the geometric majesty of the structures and the cultural elements incorporated in each building from afar. Perhaps the most architecturally fascinating element of the city is El Castillo, or the Temple of Kukulkan, devoted to a Mayan deity similar to a serpent. The 98 foot high pyramid is especially captivating during the Spring and Autumn equinoxes when the 53° angle of the structure creates shadows that mimic the appearance of the celebrated serpent.
