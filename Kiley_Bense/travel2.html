3. Dublin, Ireland: Ulysses

Every June 16th, Dublin celebrates James Joyce and his most famous work, Ulysses, by retracing the footsteps of Ulysses’ main character, Leopold Bloom. Ulysses takes place in the course of a single day in Dublin--June 16, 1904. If you visit Dublin today, you can follow Bloom’s steps in the gold plaques installed at important places from the book around the city.

2. Oxford, England: Harry Potter

Although Oxford is home to multiple Hogwarts-related sites (you can take a Harry Potter themed tour of the town), the dining hall at Christ Church College is perhaps the most spectacular. The hall--which inspired the set of Hogwarts’ Great Hall in the movies and certainly captures the spirit of the Great Hall in the books, minus the enchanted ceiling-- features gleaming candles, sweeping tables and a giant portrait of its patron, King Henry VIII.
